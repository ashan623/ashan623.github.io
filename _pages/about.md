# Research

My current research focuses on building more reliable and efficient retrieval systems for complex, multi-condition, and multimodal scenarios. I am particularly interested in the following directions:

- **Fine-grained retrieval and efficient reranking:**  
  I study how retrieval and reranking models handle complex user queries involving multiple conditions, subtle semantic differences, and hard negatives. My recent work investigates tool retrieval, document reranking, and the efficiency-performance trade-off of LLM-based rankers.

- **Omni-modality embeddings:**  
  I am interested in unified embedding models that can support retrieval across text, image, video, audio, visual documents, tools, and agent-oriented tasks. My recent work contributes to MMEB-V3, a benchmark for measuring the performance gaps of omni-modality embedding models.

- **Agentic search and decision-making:**  
  I am also interested in retrieval systems for agentic workflows, including tool retrieval, memory retrieval, multi-round information seeking, and evidence-based decision-making with external environments.

Overall, my research aims to understand the limitations of current retrieval and embedding models, and to develop more robust methods for complex real-world search scenarios.

📬 *I am open to collaborations and discussions. Please feel free to reach out if you are interested in retrieval, multimodal embeddings, reranking, or agentic search.*
